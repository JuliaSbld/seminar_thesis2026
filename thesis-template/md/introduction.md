# Introduction

Smartphone usage times of several hours each day are common in society, and mobile phones have become a central element of everyday life for large parts of the population, including but not limited to adolescents and young adults.[@bradley_stress_2023]
The Effects of the high prominence of these mobile digital devices are highly debated among scholars as well as users.
Negative effects of high usage times entail a higher risk for dementia, shorter attention spans, and mental health concerns like depression or addiction.[@eikelboom_digital_2022], [@neophytou_effects_2021], [@sigman_screen_2017]
Yet, other scholars conducting meta-analyses of a multitude of studies investigating these effects conclude minimal or non-significant negative effects of mobile digital device usage on users' health.[@odgers_annual_2020], [@orben_screens_2019], [@bradley_stress_2023]

This field of tension can be navigated more clearly when different positions are more clearly contextualized.
Effects discussed can be on the physical or mental health of users, making up entirely different fields of research.
Further, the effects can be caused by the time spent using the device and therefore not spending the time in a different manner, or by the content consumed during the usage.
This distinction is often discussed only briefly, if it is discussed at all.
Positive effects of certain usage behaviors are undermined when a simple reduction in usage time is recommended to minimize negative effects.[@marciano_digital_2022]

Therefore, a more selected usage of certain functionalities is the desired usage behavior to reduce negative effects on the mental and physical health of users.
Positive usage areas can entail interacting with others personally, listening to music or audiobooks, searching for information, navigation, or being guided through physical and mental exercises.[@linardon_efficacy_2024], [@zhang_impact_2025]
A common denominator of these functionalities is the lack of a need for a visual interface to enable them.
Whereas, visually stimulating usage areas like short video formats or pop-up notifications can be more distracting, addiction-fostering, and time-consuming for users.[@jain_exploring_2025]

That is why this paper explores the possibility of shifting away from today's mobile digital devices, which rely heavily on a visual, screen-based interaction.
I therefore propose the research question:

How does a desirable future of non-visual mobile digital device interaction look, and how do we get there?

A backcasting approach has been selected to systematically answer this research question.
This entails the design of a desirable future alongside the steps that are needed to achieve said future.
To enrich both parts of the results, a student workshop has been conducted with the goal of gaining outside perspectives on the desirability of the proposed future as well as the steps needed to reach it.

The future scenario entails the widespread use of a new mobile technology consisting of an audio output and input piece (e.g. earbud or cochlear implant) and touch sensors offering the possibility of text input via the user's hand using the Lorm alphabet.
This audio and sensory interaction-based technology is a desirable alternative to today's mainly visual mobile devices, because of its more focused approach to support the everyday life of its users.

Areas needed to be considered in the implementation and roll out of this technology are the product design, the learning of lorming and the convincing of users to switch to the technology at hand.
Therefore, hard and software advances, a widespread offering of learning tools for the Lorm alphabet, and a systematic exposure and marketing of the new technology to the public would be necessary steps to take.

Following in the next chapter is an overview of the field of tension introduced, as well as a brief explanation of the Lorm alphabet.
Chapter three gives more insights into the backcasting approach and the student workshop conducted within this research.
Results of this research are presented and discussed with respect to its implications for theoretical and practical endeavors, its limitations, and future research in chapters four and five.
The paper is concluded with a short summarization of the research.
