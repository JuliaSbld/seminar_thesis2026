# Conclusion

This seminar paper explores the effects of AI disclosure in social media posts on the message credibility of the conveyed content.
Three disclosure levels (none, partial, full) are used as independent variables in the research model, which influence the message credibility.
Mediation through perceived AI usage, as well as critical media literacy and AI attitude, is evaluated as a possible moderator of the relationship.
Hypotheses are tested using an experiment (n=19), which offers dummy social media posts as stimuli.

Results show significantly worse message credibility when full AI usage is disclosed ( $\beta$ = -1.547, p < .001), the effect is partly mediated through rising AI usage perception and non-significantly amplified in individuals with higher critical media literacy ( $\beta$ = +0.607, p = .143).
When partial AI usage is disclosed, no significant overall effect on message credibility is notable.
Yet by introducing perceived AI usage as a mediator an inconsistent mediation shows, that the disclosure itself rises message credibility ( $\beta$ = +1.294, 95% CI [+0.773, +1.840], p < .001) but by increasing perceived AI usage, which lowers message credibility ($\beta$ = -0.30, p < .001, 95% CI [-0.42, -0.19])) the effect is brought down again.

These results are in line with prior research in the field of journalism regarding the positive effect of partial AI disclosure as well as the negative effect of full AI disclosure [@chung_disclose_2026].
Yet, the inconsistent mediation of partial disclosure offers an interesting new insight that should be explored further.
Empirical reasons for the effect could offer insights for content creators on how to ideally disclose the usage of AI to minimize negative effects on message credibility perception.

Overall, the research presented is limited by a small number of participants and some technical boundaries of the analysis.
Nonetheless, some interesting findings could be presented, which ideally should be further investigated in a more rigorous research in terms of resources.
This could deepen the understanding of the underlying effects of AI disclosure in the context of social media and dissect them even further.
