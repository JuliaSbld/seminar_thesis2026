# Conclusion

This paper discusses a potential future of auditory mobile technologies to overcome negative impacts of todays visual mobile technology usage.

A backcasting approach is used to formulate a desirable future of a widespread use of mainly auditory and haptic mobile technology interaction.
Further, the steps needed to achieve this future are discussed.
Both parts of the backcasting approach are enriched by the opinions gathered within a student workshop.

Results show a moderately high desirability of the proposed future, which ultimately consists of a ear piece receiving and sending auditory signals from and to the user as well as a set of hands sensors receiving and processing signals of lorm alphabet input on the hand of the user.
Further, the main areas of steps needed to be taken to achieve said future have been found to be the technical improvement of the technologies components, the widespread learning of the lorm alphabet as well as convincing potential users to integrate the technology into their daily lifes.
