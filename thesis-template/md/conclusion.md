# Conclusion

This paper discusses a normative future of auditory mobile technologies to overcome the negative impacts of today's visual mobile technology usage, while still enabling functionalities seen as having primarily positive effects.

A backcasting approach is used to formulate a desirable future of the widespread use of mainly auditory and haptic mobile technology interaction as well as the steps needed to achieve this future.
Both parts of the backcasting approach are enriched by the opinions gathered within a student workshop, consisting of five university students in the field of information systems.

Results show a moderately high desirability of the proposed future, which ultimately consists of an earpiece receiving and sending auditory signals from and to the user, as well as a set of hand sensors receiving and processing signals of Lorm alphabet input on the hand of the user.
Further, the main areas of steps required to achieve said future have been found to be the technical improvement of the technology's components, the widespread learning of the Lorm alphabet, as well as convincing potential users to integrate the technology into their daily lives.

The main contribution of this research is the envisioned technology, which positions itself as a possible solution for promoting a more mindful and less intrusive way of integrating digital technologies into our daily lives.
Offering this solution for visual distractions and addiction-fostering applications is a new perspective for IS scholars discussing these effects.

Yet, the field of tension this solution is based upon is still in need of more theoretical contributions, discussing not only if but which applications, usage patterns, or content have a positive or negative effect on users' mental and physical health.
Even though a purely auditory and haptic technology is far away from the mobile technology usage today, developers should reflect upon the possibility of less visually (over-)stimulating applications to foster the reduction of screen time.
This would give users the opportunity to reduce negative effects of their mobile phone usage, while still being able to profit from positive effects of digital solutions.
