# Theoretical Background

In order to motivate the following backcasting approach and the future scenario presented, a presentation of the current state of discussion of technology usage as well as an introduction to the lorm alphabet is necessary.

## Technology Usage

The interaction with screen-based mobile devices e.g. smartphones is a dominant aspect of todays technology usage.
In this thesis I will use the taxonomy of @montag_how_2020 to clearly express the behavior discussed.
Here, Internet use disorder (IUD), which is an umbrella term over classified disorders enabled by digital devices, is split into predominantly mobile IUD and predominantly non-mobile IUD.

The effects of high usage times of mobile devices, especially including social media is a field of tension within the literature.
While some scholars present detrimental effects on the physical and mental well-being of users with high usage times [@eikelboom_digital_2022], others argue little to none measurable effects across studies [@odgers_annual_2020], [@orben_screens_2019]
It is to be noted here, that the topic is investigated from different fields, mainly including psychology and neuroscience.

Neuroscience scholars presented a multitude of negative effects, stemming from Internet use disorders.
These include alterations of brain matter, suggesting higher rates in early stage dementia from excessive screen exposure [@eikelboom_digital_2022], [@neophytou_effects_2021].
Further, lowered self-esteem, increases in mental health issues and addiction as well as slower learning and acquisition are suggested in adolescents and young adults.[@neophytou_effects_2021]
The finding of increases in addiction is supported, when taking into account that videogame playing is associated with similar magnitude of dopamine release as drug usage. [@weinstein_neurobiological_2020]

The argument of differing usage of mobile devices adds an important depth to the discussion [@marciano_digital_2022].
Even within one platform, e.g. Instagram, TikTok or YouTube the content consumed by users can vary to a great magnitude.
While some content might raise feelings of fear of missing out, heightened isolation or lower self-esteem, other content can lower feelings of stress and loneliness [@marciano_digital_2022].
Further, one-to-one communication as well as communities enabled by digital devices offer a way to gain positive effects of human interaction in an online setting (?).

Overall, negative effects of certain usage and content exposure are important to be aware of, yet neutral or positive effects can not be left unconsidered.
A promotion of content, interaction and usage of digital devices promoting these positive effects, while lowering exposure and over usage of harmful content and usage patterns is a sensible approach to move within this field of scholarly tension.

## Non-visual technology usage

Many digital devices used today rely heavily on visual stimuli conveyed on screens.

This focus can be questioned, when considering arguments for more tactile interactions on the well-being of consumers.
@barrios-oneill_exploring_2022 argue that the high tactile sensibility of our hands is in need of stimulation to promote well-being.
Some tactile stimulations can for example improve states of mindfulness and attention.[@stanko-kaczmarek_effects_2016]
Yet, screens offer little tactile stimulation and variety.
Moreover, an over representation of vision compared to other sensory modalities is present in other areas of research [@hutmacher_why_2019].
When considering this focus on vision based interaction as well as missing positive effects of tactile stimulation, the question for alternative technology interaction can be raised.

An already present interaction is the auditory interaction used in for example smart home devices.






## Lorm Alphabet

The lorm alphabet is a system of mapping the letters of the latin alphabet to the hand.
Due to its origin in austria, the main group of users are german speaking germans and austrians.
It uses touch motions to communicate these letters.
For that the hand of usually a deaf-blind person is touched by a communication partner.
Gestures like tapping and stroking different areas of the hand correspond to different letters, resulting in building sentences letter by letter (see figure \ref{fig:1.1}).[@schmidt_zoning-based_2016]

![Lorm Alphabet \label{fig:1.1}](figs/Lormalphabet.svg){width=40%}

This is a relevant difference to sign language used by deaf individuals, which is not a letter by letter translation of the spoken language.
Therefore, the lorm alphabet can be viewed closer to a mean of input than its own language.

### Learning

This difference is mainly important for the learning and application possibilities of lorming.
While the language most spoken using the lorm alphabet is german, using it to convey other languages which use the latin alphabet in writing is possible.
When learning to lorm only the position of corresponding letters in the hand combined with the assigned motion of touch need to be learned.

Attempts like the LeLorm App show the feasibility of designing a learning aid of the alphabet as well as a positive user study of sighted and hearing individuals with no prior knowledge of lorming.
In less than 30 minutes, three participants were able to learn corresponding motions to the letters of the alphabet with accuracies above 95%.[@schmidt_zoning-based_2016]

### Usage

As a mean of communication the lorm alphabet is used by deaf-blind individuals as well as caretakers, family members or translators.
Due to the focus of users of the lorm alphabet in german speaking countries and the relatively small number of deaf-blind individuals it is rather unknown.
The missing ability of hearing and sighted individuals to communicate with deaf-blind individuals leads to the isolation and exclusion of these individuals from many parts of society [@hersh_deafblind_2013].

Overall, it can be noted that basic knowledge of the lorm alphabet is feasible to acquire for hearing and sighted individuals and the knowledge of such could bring great benefits for deaf-blind individuals.
