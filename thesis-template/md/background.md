## Lorm Alphabet

The deaf-blind austrian poet and philosophical writer, Hieronymus Lorm, has invented the lorm alphabet in 18xx.
It uses touch to convey the letters of the latin alphabet.
For that the hand of usually a deaf-blind person is touched by a communication partner.
Gestures like tapping and stroking different areas of the hand correspond to different letters, resulting in building sentences letter by letter.(Schmidt et al.)
This is a relevant difference to sign language used by deaf individuals, which is not a letter by letter translation of the spoken language.
Therefore, the lorm alphabet can be viewed closer to a mean of input than its own language.
