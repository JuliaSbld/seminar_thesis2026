The desirability can be questioned in several aspects, including the focus on auditory communication and the inclusion of the lorm alphabet.

## Future Research

Several directions of action can be taken from this thesis.

Grounded in the small population of lorm alphabet users, the research regarding learning it is slim.
Especially for sighted and hearing individuals, a systematic study of duration, and difficulties of learning to use the lorm alphabet would be of great value to the thoughts presented in this paper.
That is, because the ease of learning to lorm is an important factor in the ease of using the entire proposed technology.

Furthermore, another interesting endeavour could be a prototyping study, trying to use technologies available today to make a physical prototype of the proposed technology.
This would give a much more tangible option to find flaws and opportunities of the idea compared to a purely theoretical model.

Another field in need of a closer investigation is the impact of auditory technologies on areas negatively impacted by their visual counterparts.
Due to the high preference of discussing vision compared to other sensory modalities (Hutmacher), as well as the high occurrence of visual interaction in technologies (?) this focus on auditory technologies is underrepresented.
A systematic comparison between effects of visual technologies and auditory technologies would give a more stable base for the question of which route is more beneficial on the mental and physical health of users.
