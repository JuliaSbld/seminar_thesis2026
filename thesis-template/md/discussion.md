# Discussion

## Implications for the Theory

The positive effect of partial self-disclosure, as already discussed in prior research [@chung_disclose_2026], is supported by my data.
Testing the mediation through perceived AI Usage brings even stronger results by showing the inconsistent mediation of message credibility in that context.
The rise of message credibility through partial AI disclosure despite the weakening effect of heightened AI perception is an interesting finding within this research.
It implies complex, contradicting effects present in the context of AI disclosure in the investigated field of Social Media.

Another addition to existing research in the field is the amplification of the effects of AI disclosure in individuals with higher critical media literacy.
The finding here clearly contradicts the moderation hypothesis made beforehand.
Participants with higher Critical Media Literacy reacted more strongly to disclosure cues, which shows that the label is not questioned as anticipated, but rather more strongly taken into consideration.

## Implications for the Practice

The strongest implications for practical applications of AI disclosure in the context of social media are that partial self-disclosure seems to aid message credibility, if the perception of AI involvement is still kept low.
Therefore, if AI has been used for parts of the posts, it is sensible to disclose the partial involvement to show transparency about the content.
In the low-stakes environment of Social Media, this research shows that partial disclosure is not penalized by users.
Nonetheless, disclosing full involvement of AI in the creation of content is heavily penalized with lower message credibility perception.

## Limitations

Several limitations can be noted about the research presented within this seminar paper.

First and foremost, the small sample size of participants in the experiment limits generalizability and makes the research more exploratory than definitive.
Nonetheless, interesting results with significant statistical effects were reached and can be taken seriously in this limited context.
Furthermore, the sample itself was skewed towards younger, more educated individuals, which can further limit the results to this sample.
This non-representative sample is mainly rooted in the sourcing strategy used.

Another limitation within this research that should be briefly noted is the stimuli created for the experiment.
While the dummy Social Media posts were deliberately designed without an author, a source, and in the same general style to make the message the point of focus for participants, it cannot be claimed that the posts were entirely identical in the degree of inherent message credibility.
The statements shown could be rated as differently credible without taking AI involvement and disclosure into account.
Therefore, the rated message credibility could also be partly influenced by the nature of the message itself.

In line with this, the path direction of perceived AI Usage and message credibility can not clearly be confirmed.
It is possible, within the conducted experiment, that the rating of message credibility influenced the perceived AI usage as well, because both are measured at the same time.
Therefore, the direction of the b-path in the research model is assumed and has not been further confirmed in this research.

At last, a more technical limitation of the analysis is also relevant to this research.
The more credible approach for the within-subject experiment of using a mixed linear model has been implemented when possible.
An exception here is the mediation analysis, because the statsmodels mediation class used does not support mixed models.
For this part of the analysis, an OLS regression with 1000 bootstrap replications has been used.
The non-independence of observations within a single subject could therefore not be accounted for in these estimates.
This might lead to amplified results within this section and should be interpreted with appropriate caution.

## Future Research

Several interesting directions of research could follow upon this seminar paper.
The clearest one would be a revision of the experiment with a more rigorous sample size and possibly more detailed AI disclosure labels.
This would give deeper insights into the generalizability of the presented results and also explore the most interesting strand of partial AI disclosure further.
In this regard, exploring the reasons for the positive effect of partial disclosure more could hold interesting results, which would deepen the understanding of this result.
More nuanced labels could include further explained AI involvement, like designed with AI, the script has been aided by AI, or factual information has been checked by AI.
Further, the stimuli could be changed to better account for the believability of certain messages and give more variety to stabilize results further.
Overall, I’m positive that enlarging the entire research would eliminate most limitations that this approach did not have the resources to address.
