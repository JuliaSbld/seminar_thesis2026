# Results

## Effects of AI disclosure on Message Credibility

To answer the main research question, the influence of AI disclosure on message credibility has been tested using a mixed effects model.
On the one hand, results show no significant influence of partial AI disclosure on message credibility (β = +0.199, p = .425).
On the other hand, the influence of full AI disclosure is significant and shows a substantial effect ( β = -1.547, p < .001).
This shows a noticeable difference between the different levels of AI disclosure, meaning that Ai disclosure cannot be viewed as a dichotomous variable, but is in need of more precise definition, even in the context of short and informal information sharing like on Social Media.
The group variance of reported levels of message credibility is 0.917, indicating the relevance of the random intercept, which has been used exactly for the purpose of taking these between subject differences of overall message credibility perception into account.

## Disclosure x CMC Moderation

Testing a possible moderation of the effect on message credibility by the critical media literacy of participants showed no significant results (p =.229 and p =.457).
Therefore, the critical media literacy does not change the effect of AI disclosure on message credibility in a way, that is measurable in the data of this research.

## Perceived AI x Attitude Moderation

Similarly, the Attitude towards AI showed no significant effect (p = .802) on the connection of perceived AI usage and Message Credibility.
A more positive view of AI, therefore did not influence the negative effect, perceived AI usage has on Message Credibility.

## Mediation

A most certainly relevant finding of this research is the mediation chain through perceived AI usage.
To test whether the connection between AI disclosure and Message Credibility travels through perceived AI usage ... has been done.
This showed a significant effect, indicating that the AI disclosure influences perceived AI usage first, which then shows and effect on message credibility.

## Demographics

Age as the most interpretable variable shows no significant effect on the reported connection of perceived AI usage and Message Credibility.
The same holds for gender, where men reported slightly higher message credibility scores overall.



## CMC and perceived AI usage

At last, the proposed hypothesis of Critical Media Literacy influencing the connection of AI disclosure and perceived AI usage has been tested.
Results show an effect of CML on this connection in the opposite way, that the hypothesis stated.
High CML participants seem to not question, but to amplify the cue that they are given.
Meaning, no disclosure leads to lower perceived AI usage and disclosure leads to higher perceived AI usage.
