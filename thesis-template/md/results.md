# Results

The final goal to be achieved within the timeframe of fifty years is defined as a predominant use of the audio focused mobile technology.
This technology in its final form entails an implant, which is able to stimulate the audio reception of users as well as implanted sensors in one hand, which are able to detect touch.
Users can interact with the device with spoken word or lorming, which is detected via the hand sensors.
Artificial Intelligence enables natural feeling interaction with the device, which is able to carry out a multitude of functionalities currently enabled by smartphones.
Examples are, receiving and sending messages and calls, searching for information, listening to music, audio books, podcasts and other audio input formats as well as setting alarms and reminders.
Many more use cases are feasible to be supported by the technology.

## Steps to be taken

Achieving this goal relies on several dimensions of change, which have been identified using the input of the conducted student workshop.
Each aspect should be aided by a specific roadmap, while recognizing their interdependencies.

### Product design

To address the necessary achievements in functionalities and product design, the proposed technology can be separated into four parts.
First, is the audio out- and input.
An immediately available example of this part are earbuds, currently used in connection with mostly mobile devices.
When considering these models for the proposed technology an aspect to be improved is the battery life.
Current models have an average working time of x hours, before needing to be charged, which would be a problem for a continuos use in everyday life.
Another technology to be considered in the future could be implants.
Cochlear implants, which are used today to stimulate the x nerve of individuals with hearing loss, would be a viable option here.


Second of all, the software supporting the technology has to be developed.
This includes connections to use existing applications using the input of voice and lorming as well as developing an overall structure for the screenless interaction.
The vision for the interaction is also highly dependant on natural responses when seeking information, reading messages and navigating the technology.
Therefore, accurate and natural responses form Large language models (LLMs) used in the technology are also necessary.
Current advances in LLMs can be considered sufficient for the interaction needed.

When discussing the third part, the hardware providing the computing power for the device, several steps can be considered.
The computing power needed to provide functionalities similar to these of todays smartphones // research sizes.
Therefore, an immediate prototype of ear bud sized devices with such computing power are not feasible.
An external device providing the functionality would be needed and connected to the in- and output means.
Yet, based in Moore's Law an ongoing reduction in chip sizes is realistic, which offers a clear vision of the feasibility to have small enough hardware to support the computing power needed within ear buds or an implant.

The last part are the sensors, which detect the tactile input of the user.
Current research already suggests gloves, which are able to detect inputs of the lorm alphabet.[@ozioko_smart_2022], [@luo_adaptive_2024]
Other options for flexible tactile sensors can also be explored, when considering to design less visually noticeable options. [@pyo_recent_2021]

Overall, current technologies already offer a very strong basis needed for the development of the proposed technology.
Further advancements in the size and subtlety of the components is realistic given the timeframe discussed within this backcasting approach.
Therefore, the product design is not perceived as the biggest challenge for reaching the desired future.
This is also reflected within the student workshop in which aspects of product design are mentioned in three out of four answers for the question of which steps are needed to implement the idea.
Yet, none responses to which is the biggest challenge in the implementation mention product design aspects.


### Learning lorming

In order to use the technology to its full potential, mastering lorming is a key aspect.
This ensures a fast and non-intrusive mean of input.
Therefore learning lorming is one of the key challenges to address, when rolling out the technology.

The process of learning can be divided into getting familiar with the structure including to remember the mapping of letters to hand regions and gestures by heart and practicing the usage of this mapping.
Tools like learning apps for the lorm alphabet can enable this learning, especially in the early stages.[@schmidt_zoning-based_2016]
Based on @schmidt_zoning-based_2016 a single session of familiarization with the alphabet can be enough to internalize the mapping.
The bigger challenge is the longer practice period needed to be able to use lorming in a way which is a substitute for the spoken language.

Looking at the technology proposal at hand, building in a learning tool that gives auditory feedback about the correctness of input via the hand sensors is a sensible approach.
Because the technology is not entirely dependent on lorming, yet beneficial for certain use cases, learning lorming with the help of the technology is plausible.

### Convincing Users

'The only thing actually missing is the acceptance of society.'

Convincing users to use the new technology has been identified as the key challenge by all workshop participants.



## Desirability

Considering the normative nature of the backcasting approach used, examining the perceived desirability of the proposed future scenario is necessary.
During the student workshop interesting perspectives on this matter have been collected.
Overall, 75% of workshop participants stated to perceive the proposed technology as desirable.

Positive aspects of the technology identified during this research entail a more mindful interaction with technology, high accessibility and compatibility with existing technologies.
The first aspect is mainly enabled by less distractions when interacting with the technology compared to a smartphone.
Visual cues on the smartphone like notifications and app symbols do not exist in the proposed technology, reducing chronic sensory stimulation, which is argued to increase the risk of several mental disorder. [@eikelboom_digital_2022]
Another supporting argument is an increase of engagement with the world around users.
When using a screen-based mobile device, the visual processing of the outside world decreases more compared to interacting with technology solely through audio. (Quelle?)
In addition, mental health enablers like guided breathing and meditation exercises, music therapy as well as documenting thoughts is still enabled by the technology. (Quelle für positive Effekte davon)

Another desirable aspect is the accessibility of the technology in respect to blind and potentially also deaf-blind users.
The lack of a visual component makes the ability to see irrelevant in the usage.
An extension of the hand sensors to also answer the user using impulses in the structure of the lorm alphabet substitutes the audio aspect of the technology.
Furthermore, cochlear implants can be used as the mean of audio output and have shown to be helpful to deaf-blind users as well. [@daneshi_outcome_2022]
Aside from the accessibility of the technology, the societal implications could be of much greater value to deaf and deaf-blind individuals.
When reaching the desired proposed future, a high percentage of able-bodied individuals would be able to use the lorm alphabet.
Even without interacting through the technology, interaction with deaf and deaf-blind individuals would become possible for many more people.
Therefore, the proposed future would not only unlock a new way of technology interaction, but also reduce communication barriers and the isolation of deaf-blind individuals.[@hersh_deafblind_2013]

The technology supports everyday tasks, including communication through calls and messages, seeking information, supporting memory through lists and notes, and listening to news, music, podcasts or audio books.
Therefore, offering a similar range of functionalities as smartphones today.
It is not dependant on a critical amount of users, because of its compatibility with existing technologies.

Nonetheless, negative aspects of the technology must also be taken into consideration when discussing the desirability of the proposed future.
The two main arguments made by workshop participants are the steep learning curve to use the technology as well as slower interaction with the technology compared to smartphones.
The steep learning curve stems from the need to learn the lorm alphabet to interact with the technology outside of speech.
